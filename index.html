In school I was always watching the teachers and the decisions they made. I was curious how and why they taught the way they did. I dreamt of a better version of school. After high school I went to Bennington College in Vermont. It was a lawless institution that allowed me to create my own BA in Education and Writing for Performance (mostly plays, screenplays, and standup). While there I worked with the local school's at-risk student population and was employed in a psychiatric hospital. When I graduated I moved to Portland, where I worked with homeless adolescents and young adults.
